Sultan Salahuddin Al-Ayyubi

The Noble Who Reclaimed Jurusalem

The great time line of Salahuddin Al-Ayyubi's life:

  • 1137/1138 – Born in Tikrit, Iraq, to a Kurdish family.
  • 1146 – Moves with family to Mosul, then later to Syria in service of Nur ad-Din, the ruler of Aleppo.
  • 1164 – 1169 – Participates in military campaigns in Egypt under Nur ad-Din’s command.
  • 1169 – Becomes vizier of Egypt, serving under the Fatimid Caliphate.
  • 1171 – Abolishes the Fatimid Caliphate in Egypt and restores Sunni rule under the Abbasid Caliphate.
  • 1174 – Nur ad-Din dies; Salahuddin moves to take control of Damascus and begins uniting Muslim lands.
  • 1174 – 1186 – Gradually gains control over Syria, Yemen, parts of Iraq, and the Hijaz.
  • 1187 (July 4) – Wins the Battle of Hattin, crushing Crusader forces.
  • 1187 (October 2) – Recaptures Jerusalem from the Crusaders.
  • 1189 – 1192 – Faces the Third Crusade, led by Richard the Lionheart; both sides respect each other but Jerusalem remains under Muslim control.
  • 1192 – Signs a truce with the Crusaders allowing Christian pilgrims access to Jerusalem.
  • 1193 (March 4) – Dies in Damascus, leaving behind a united Muslim state and a legendary legacy.
